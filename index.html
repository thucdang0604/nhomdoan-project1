Concept

Calligraphy is a word derived from ancient Greek, composed of two words κάλλος - kallos and γραφή - graphẽ. They mean beauty and text, when combined can be understood as creating beauty for text by combining lines and images in a reasonable way.

In Vietnamese, we have a word used to refer to Calligraphy, which is Thư Pháp. Therefore, Calligraphy font can be simply understood as calligraphy fonts with the most recognizable feature being that they all have beautiful winding lines.

Calligraphy in culture differs greatly from the usual writing style as it requires the use of a feather pen, so you can easily see the letters that are usually large and gradually smaller towards the end of the letter. The most popular modern Calligraphy font today is Copperplate with slender lines, creating a modern but still very elegant and artistic feeling. You can easily catch handwritten Calligraphy fonts on wedding cards or even at weddings.

The word "Hỷ" written in Calligraphy font is always pasted everywhere in weddings and the names of the bride and groom use Copperplate fonts. This is almost considered an unwritten rule of all weddings, showing that the influence of calligraphy is very great. Today, with the development of technology, Calligraphy fonts have been Vietnameseized and extremely diverse in style for designers to freely use.

History

Calligraphy has a long history of formation and development, spanning from East to West. It plays a role as part of the development of human civilization, always having a close relationship with our lives, making Calligraphy always developing and never forgotten or discarded.

There is much evidence that the formation of handwritten Calligraphy fonts originated in the West when the Latin alphabet was born in Rome and in the year 600 BC. You can see that the lines of classic Western Calligraphy fonts have many straight lines interspersed with curved lines, quite different from later fonts.

In the East, Calligraphy is expressed through the calligraphy art of China, Japan and Korea with the pictographic Chinese character system. Vietnam is also one of the countries that uses Chinese Calligraphy. Contrary to the West, Eastern Calligraphy places heavy emphasis on criteria such as paper quality, ink, pen, ink concentration...

However, it is not to say that Eastern Calligraphy only values external factors but the technique of the writer also plays an extremely important role. The force of pressing and releasing of the writer, the tilt force of the pen... must be tightly controlled to create a true calligraphy work. Today, calligraphy in Vietnam is still maintained, typically the culture of asking for letters at the beginning of the year.

How to Write and Pen

Calligraphy is the art of creating with writing and partly with images to create a work of aesthetic, uniform and harmonious. To start writing Calligraphy, you need to prepare some necessary tools such as notebooks, pens and washi stickers.

There are many different types of Calligraphy pens and you can choose the type of pen that suits your level and needs. Some common Calligraphy pens for beginners include Pilot Parallel Calligraphy, Tombow Fudenosuke, Zebra Disposable Brush, Sakura Koi Brush Pen, Tombow Dual Brush AB-T, Pentel Fude Touch Brush and Crayola Super Tips.

When writing Calligraphy, you need to practice basic strokes such as upstrokes, downstrokes, up curves, down curves, oval strokes, top loops and bottom loops. When writing upstrokes you need to lightly touch the pen to make the stroke thinner. Downstrokes are the opposite, requiring more force to produce a thicker stroke. You should hold the pen at a 45 degree angle to the paper for easier manipulation.

You can learn more about how to write Calligraphy through online tutorials or by taking Calligraphy writing courses.

Benefit

Learning to write Calligraphy has many benefits for the learner. It is a healthy hobby and helps you relax, reduce stress and fatigue. It also helps you accumulate more knowledge about culture and practice perseverance and focus.

In addition, learning to write Calligraphy also helps you join a community of people who love writing and share your passion with those who have the same interests.

Types of Calligraphy


Traditional Calligraphy

Calligraphy is the art of creating beautiful writing. It originated from China and was introduced to Vietnam in the early years of the Han dynasty.

The first calligraphic work of Vietnam found was the line "Đại Tuỳ Cửu Chân quận Bảo An đạo tràng chi bi văn" written in the "triện" style on the forehead of a stele in Trường Xuân village (Thanh Hóa).

Contemporary Calligraphy

Calligraphy has evolved over time and has taken on different forms. In addition to traditional calligraphy, there is also contemporary calligraphy and modern calligraphy.

These forms of calligraphy use different styles and techniques to create beautiful writing.

Modern Calligraphy

Calligraphy comes from Ancient Greek, including κάλλος - kallos meaning "beauty" and γραφή graphê "text", i.e. the visual art associated with writing.

Modern Calligraphy is simply understood as Modern Calligraphy, which is Calligraphy which is not performed in the traditional way. However, the most basic part of Modern Calligraphy is based on traditional Calligraphy.

Handwriting and Design

Handwriting and design are also important aspects of calligraphy. The tools used, such as pens and ink, play a crucial role in creating beautiful writing.

Calligraphers must also have a deep understanding of how to combine and distribute shapes, create character shapes through each stroke, and have a solid foundation of knowledge to transcribe.
