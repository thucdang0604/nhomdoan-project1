CONCEPTION

Calligraphy is a word derived from Ancient Greek, compounded by two words κάλλος - kallos and γραφή - graphê. They mean beauty and text, when put together can be understood in the sense of creating beauty for text by combining lines and images in a reasonable way

In Vietnamese, we have a word used to refer to Calligraphy, which is Calligraphy. Therefore, Calligraphy fonts can simply be understood as calligraphic fonts with the most obvious point being that they all have beautiful flowing lines.

Calligraphy in the culture is completely different from the normal writing style when it requires the writer to use a brush, so you can also easily notice the large and smaller lowercase strokes towards the end of the letter. .

The most popular modern Calligraphy font today is the Copperplate typeface with slender lines, creating a modern feeling but still very polite and artistic. You can easily find handwritten Calligraphy fonts on wedding invitations or even in weddings. The word “Hí” written in Calligraphy font is always pasted everywhere at the wedding and the bride and groom’s names are used in Copperplate fonts. This is almost an unwritten rule of every wedding, showing that the influence of calligraphy is huge. Today, with the development of technology, Calligraphy fonts have been Vietnameseized and extremely diverse designs for designers to freely use.

HISTORY

Calligraphy has a very long history of formation and development, spanning from East to West. It plays a role as a part of the development of human civilization, always closely related to our lives, making Calligraphy always develop, not be forgotten or eliminated.

There is much evidence for the formation of handwritten Calligraphy fonts from the West when the Latin alphabet was born in Rome and 600 BC. You may notice that the lines of the classic Western Calligraphy font have many straight lines interspersed with curved strokes, which is quite different from later fonts.

In the East, Calligraphy is expressed through the calligraphy art of China, Japan and Korea with the hieroglyphic kanji system. Vietnam is also one of the countries that use Chinese Calligraphy. Contrary to the West, Eastern Calligraphy places heavy emphasis on the quality of paper, ink, pens, and the density of the mixed ink…

However, it is not that Eastern Calligraphy only emphasizes but the external factors that the writer's technique also plays a very important role. The force of pressing and letting go of the writer, the tilting force of the pen... need to be strictly controlled to form a real calligraphic work. Today, calligraphy in Vietnam is still maintained, typically the culture of asking for letters at the beginning of the year.

Benefit

Learning to write Calligraphy has many benefits for the learner. It is a healthy hobby and helps you relax, reduce stress and fatigue. It also helps you accumulate more knowledge about culture and practice perseverance and focus.

In addition, learning to write Calligraphy also helps you join a community of people who love writing and share your passion with those who have the same interests.

Types of Calligraphy


Traditional Calligraphy

Calligraphy is the art of creating beautiful writing. It originated from China and was introduced to Vietnam in the early years of the Han dynasty.

The first calligraphic work of Vietnam found was the line "Đại Tuỳ Cửu Chân quận Bảo An đạo tràng chi bi văn" written in the "triện" style on the forehead of a stele in Trường Xuân village (Thanh Hóa).

Contemporary Calligraphy

Calligraphy has evolved over time and has taken on different forms. In addition to traditional calligraphy, there is also contemporary calligraphy and modern calligraphy.

These forms of calligraphy use different styles and techniques to create beautiful writing.

Modern Calligraphy

Calligraphy comes from Ancient Greek, including κάλλος - kallos meaning "beauty" and γραφή graphê "text", i.e. the visual art associated with writing.

Modern Calligraphy is simply understood as Modern Calligraphy, which is Calligraphy which is not performed in the traditional way. However, the most basic part of Modern Calligraphy is based on traditional Calligraphy.

Handwriting and Design

Handwriting and design are also important aspects of calligraphy. The tools used, such as pens and ink, play a crucial role in creating beautiful writing.

Calligraphers must also have a deep understanding of how to combine and distribute shapes, create character shapes through each stroke, and have a solid foundation of knowledge to transcribe.
